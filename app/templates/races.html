About the F1 Calendar

The Formula 1 season typically consists of around 20-24 races held across different countries and continents. The calendar begins in March and concludes in December, with races usually held every two to three weeks.

Each race weekend consists of practice sessions on Friday, qualifying on Saturday, and the main race on Sunday. Points are awarded to the top 10 finishers in each race, with the driver and team with the most points at the end of the season crowned World Champions.
